WIG hair Types

6A Virgin Hair

Consists of human hair derived from more than two people. Despite the virgin hair having been cut from different people, the weave is still in the same direction. However, due to the differences between people’s hair, the hair must undergo special processing. This type of hair delivers a softer, silkier feel when brushed straight and following several shampoo washes allowing for nutrition absorption. Virgin hair weft closely adheres to natural human hair. Significantly, virgin hair can be washed, dyed, and permed many times. Our premium quality hair weaves are washable up to 20 times without shedding.

6A+ 100% Unprocessed Virgin Hair

Made from a single person human hair bulk, this premium quality hair is pure and entirely natural for maximum luxury. Instead of being machined, this hair only needs to be combed by a wood board in the factory before it’s ready for sale; this allows for a uniformly thick and naturally straight composition without using any type of chemical treatment. Additionally, the straight style feels silky smooth for a stunning effect. The natural human hair allows it to be washed, dyed, and permed many times ensuring optimum results each time. 6A+ 100% Unprocessed Virgin Hair is also difficult to tangle and benefits from having a longer life than ordinary braids (around 5-10 times longer).

5A Remy Hair

Collected from more than one person, the thickness of each hair strand is not uniform or regular. The hair cuticle is removed due to chemical processing so that the consistency of the head and tail of each hair strand is reversed. Without the cuticle, the hair can become thinner and softer, leading to a higher probability of tangling. The 5A Remy Hair can feel a little rough, especially the lighter colors, and compared to virgin hair, can appear to be not as bright. Dark color Remy Hair is washable up to 15 times without any shedding while the lighter colors can be washed between 5 and 10 times before shedding.

Q: What kind of wigs should I choose: synthetic or human hair?
A:
Thanks to advanced manufacturing techniques, the quality of synthetic wigs is exceptional and visually indistinguishable from human hair, even for sharp-eyed experts. Synthetic wigs have the advantage of being easier to care for as well as being priced at a much lower level. The mono-filament fiber "hair" used to make synthetic wigs also a form of “memory”, meaning that it maintains its wave or curl, along with specific texture and volume; this ensures that there is minimal upkeep. Synthetic wigs, however, are not designed to accept a permanent wave or "hair" dye, and can be permanently damaged by the use of curling irons, blow dryers or hot rollers.
Q: What type of fiber is used for your synthetic wigs or hairpieces?
A:
The fiber is made from a special heat resistant raw material, imported from Japan. It is commonly used for hair extensions, weaves, wigs and doll’s hair. It is flame-retardant and comes in a variety of styles and textures, and has the benefit of being able to be styled more frequently than human hair.
Q: How long do you expect the wig to last?
A:
Generally, if you do not wear your wig on a daily basis, and take good care of it, synthetic hair wigs can last for over 1 year. Lace front wigs can last even longer, as the hair is carefully needled to the lace by hand. In terms of 100% human hair wigs, those can last for over 3 years with proper maintenance.

How to Take Measurements

1. Pin hair as flat and as tight as possible before taking the measurements.
2. Round your measurements up not down. Example: 12 3/4 should be rounded up to 13 and 12 1/4 should be rounded up to 12 1/2.

Note: If you are bald or do not have a hair line - you do not need to add the inch - simply put the tape measure where you would like your hairline to begin with the wig. The averages below are just examples. These measurements will not fit everyone-some may need bigger or smaller size.

1. Circumference
Measure all around the head. Position tape measure so its edge follows the hair line around the head and nape of the neck. (Average measurement is 22 inches).
2. Forehead to Nape of neck
Measure from hairline at center of forehead straight back over crown to center of hairline at nape of neck. (Average measurement is 13 1/2 inches).
3. Ear to Ear Across Front Hairline
Measure all around the head. Position tape measure so its edge follows the hair line around the head and nape of the neck. (Average measurement is 22 inches).
4. Ear to Ear Over the Top
Measure from hairline directly above the ear across top of head to the hairline directly above the other ear. (Average measurement is 11 inches).
5. Temple to Temple Around Back
From temple to temple across the back of head. (Average measurement is 14 inches).
6. Nape of Neck
Measure the width of hairline across the nape of the neck. (Average measurement is 6 inches).

Washing Tips

If you wear wigs frequently, a 10-15 day wash cycle is recommended. Please ensure you do not rub or scrub the wig during washing. Place the wig in a suitably ventilated location following washing and dry thoroughly. (DO NOT EXPOSE DIRECTLY TO THE SUN).

Cleaning Steps

Improper cleaning can damage wigs permanently. Only follow the approved washing method of washing to prolong the lifespan of the wig.

1. Preparation

Shampoo; Nursing liquid; Steel Comb; Hair Stand; Fine Comb; Basin; Warm Water.

2. Cleaning

Fill a basin with warm water (20°C / 68°F); this should not be boiling hot. Use an appropriate amount of neutral shampoo into the water, and stir carefully. Turning the net cap upside down, carefully immerse the wig into the water, and pat gently with your hand ensuring you do not scrub the wig. Afterwards, rinse the wig thoroughly with water.

3. How to Care

Fill a basin with warm water (25°C / 77°F or so), and add the appropriate amount of hair conditioner. Immerse and submerge the wig fully into the water for 5-10 minutes, before carefully removing the wig from the water using a dry towel. Place the wig securely on the hair stand to restore the wig to its original style.

4. How to Dry

Place the wig in a well-ventilated place to let it to dry naturally. When storing the wig, please remember to use a dry location to prevent damage to the wig.
